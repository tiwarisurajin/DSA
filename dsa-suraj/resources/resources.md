# Resources

Curated, not exhaustive. Everything here is here because it's genuinely worth your time, not because a "complete list" needs an entry.

## Books

- **"Cracking the Coding Interview" — Gayle Laakmann McDowell.** Still the most complete single-book interview prep resource, despite its age. Best used for the behavioral/process chapters and as a problem bank, not as your primary theory source (this repo's Handbook is meant to replace that role).
- **"Introduction to Algorithms" (CLRS) — Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein.** The canonical algorithms textbook. Dense — use it as a reference for rigorous proofs and formal complexity analysis, not a cover-to-cover read.
- **"Elements of Programming Interviews in Python" — Aziz, Lee, Prakash.** Python-specific, well-matched to this repo's stack.

## Courses

- **MIT 6.006 (Introduction to Algorithms)** — OpenCourseWare, free. The closest thing to a university-grade DSA course available publicly.
- **NeetCode's YouTube channel and NeetCode 150 list** — the problem selection is genuinely well-curated for interview-frequency, and the video explanations model the "think aloud" process well.

## Practice Platforms

- **LeetCode** — primary practice platform, matches this repo's problem numbering conventions.
- **AlgoExpert** (paid) — polished video explanations, useful if a topic in this repo still isn't clicking after the writeup + your own dry run.

## Papers / Deeper Reading

- Queued — add papers here as specific topics (e.g. hash table collision resolution strategies, B-trees) warrant primary-source reading beyond what the Handbook covers.

## How to use this list

Don't try to consume all of it. This repo (theory + patterns + problems) is designed to be sufficient on its own — use these as backup when a specific explanation isn't landing, not as a parallel curriculum.
